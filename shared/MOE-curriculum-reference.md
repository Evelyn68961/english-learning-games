# Taiwan MOE 12-Year Curriculum — English Reference

Source of truth for difficulty-tiering and theme content in `question-bank.js`.
Compiled from MOE 課綱 (108 課綱) materials, the MOE common-2000 word list, and the 大考中心 7000-word reference list (111 學年度版).

## Vocabulary structure

**Elementary + Junior High (G3–G9):** MOE 課綱 references **2000 common words** (常用2000字詞), split into:
- **Basic 1200** (基本1200字詞) — must be covered by every textbook; mandatory through G9.
- **Challenge 800** (挑戰800字詞) — supplementary; widely used in advanced junior-high material.

**Senior High (G10–G12):** 大考中心 publishes the **高中英文參考詞彙表** with **7000 words across 6 levels** (~1000 per level, L1 easiest → L6 hardest). The MOE 2000 mostly maps to L1–L2 (so we don't duplicate those in senior tiers). Senior tiers in this bank target L3 onward.

- **L1–L2:** overlap with junior 2000 — already covered by diff 3–4 in this bank
- **L3:** 高一 (G10) core — the big leap from junior
- **L4:** 高二 (G11) core — 學測 base
- **L5:** 高三 (G12) — 學測 ceiling
- **L6:** 分科測驗 (AST) — abstract, academic, post-學測 elite

## Difficulty → grade mapping used in this repo

| Bank diff | MOE grade band | Vocab pool | Grammar focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | G3 starter (pre-A1) | ~100 most iconic single-syllable items | noun ID, simple "is/are", vocab fill-in (no real grammar concept) |
| 1 | G3–G4 (A1) | basic 1200, common half | be-verb agreement, can/have, regular plural -s, articles a/the |
| 2 | G5–G6 (A2) | basic 1200, full | present continuous, this/that/these/those, do/does, simple past (regular) |
| 3 | G7–G8 (B1) | + 800 challenge, junior-only | past simple (irreg), comparative/superlative, present perfect, future will/be going to |
| 4 | G9 (B2-prep) | full 2000 + abstract | passive, conditionals 1/2, past perfect, subjunctive (I wish), reported speech |
| 5 | G10 高一 (B2) | 大考中心 L3 (~1000 senior-core) | gerund vs infinitive, full relative clauses, 12-tense matrix, advanced passive |
| 6 | G11 高二 (B2+) | 大考中心 L4 | participle phrases, inversion (Hardly/No sooner), advanced subjunctive, mixed conditionals |
| 7 | G12 學測 (C1) | 大考中心 L5 | cleft sentences, advanced reported speech, parallelism, complex causatives |
| 8 | 分科測驗 AST (C1+) | 大考中心 L6 | sophisticated register, nuanced cohesion (notwithstanding, albeit), literary devices, formal academic patterns |

## Curriculum principles (paraphrased)

- Spiral progression: each grammar topic introduced minimally, revisited and deepened across grades. Avoid exhaustive coverage on first introduction.
- Elementary stage prioritises listening + speaking, with reading/writing limited to single words and simple sentences.
- Grammar should be inferred from meaningful context first, then formalised.
- Basic and core grammar only — avoid obscure constructions in textbooks.

## Senior-high theme → eligible vocabulary (大考中心 L3–L6)

These are senior-high tiers (diff 5–8). **Source-of-truth principle:** every word listed here either (a) appears with measurable frequency on past 學測/分科 papers (105–113 學年度), or (b) sits in 大考中心 L3–L5 of the official 7000-word reference. Avoid technical jargon (-ology endings, biological taxa, chemical names) unless they actually appear on tests.

**Categories are intentionally expanded** beyond the elementary/junior noun lists, because real exams test general verbs/adjectives/abstract nouns more than theme-specific nouns. The expanded scopes:

- **animals → wildlife / ecology**
- **food → food / nutrition / agriculture**
- **colors → visual descriptions / appearance / art**
- **family → family / relationships / society**
- **school → education / learning / academic life**
- **feelings → emotions / mental states / attitudes**
- **numbers → quantity / measurement / statistics**
- **hobbies → leisure / arts / entertainment**
- **body → body / health / medicine / fitness**
- **clothes → fashion / style / appearance**
- **weather → weather / climate / environment**
- **time → time / change / sequence / history**
- **jobs → career / work / business / profession**
- **places → places / travel / geography / urban life**
- **transport → transport / movement / journey**
- **sports → sports / fitness / competition**

## Grammar item bank by difficulty

### diff 0 — vocab fill, 4-6 word sentences, jokey distractors
- "I see a ___." → noun pick (animal/food noun) vs unrelated word
- "It is a ___." → noun pick
- "[Theme noun] is ___." → adjective pick (big/small/cute/red)
- "I like ___." → theme noun (singular or plural ok)
- Theme-specific catch-phrase

### diff 1 — A1, basic structure
- be-verb agreement: is/are/am
- modal `can`: "A frog ___ jump." (can/is/are)
- regular plural: "I see two ___." (cat/cats/catting)
- article a/an: "I have ___ apple." (an/a/the) — only basic phonetic cases
- have/has: "He ___ a bag." (has/have)

### diff 2 — A2, slightly richer
- present continuous: "The dog is ___." (run/runs/running)
- this/that/these/those: "Look at ___ tigers!" (this/that/those)
- demonstrative + plural: "These ___ my pens." (is/are/am)
- do/does: "___ you like tea?" (Do/Does/Are)
- definite article: "I clean ___ table." (a/an/the)
- simple past (regular): "I ___ rice yesterday." (eat/ate/ated→eaten variants)

### diff 3 — B1, junior-only
- past simple irregular: "Yesterday I ___ a panda." (see/saw/seen)
- comparative: "A lion is ___ than a cat." (big/bigger/biggest)
- superlative: "This is the ___ pencil." (long/longer/longest)
- present perfect: "She ___ never tried surfing." (have/has/had)
- future: "I ___ visit my aunt tomorrow." (will/would/am)
- because/so basic conjunction

### diff 4 — B2-prep, advanced junior
- passive voice: "The mouse was ___ by the cat." (catch/caught/catching)
- past perfect: "When I arrived, the bird ___ flown away." (has/had/have)
- conditional 2: "If I were a fish, I ___ swim." (will/would/can)
- subjunctive: "I wish I ___ a pet panda." (have/had/having)
- reported speech: "He said he ___ a horse." (rode/rides/will ride)
- modals of deduction: "He must ___ eaten the food." (has/have/had)

### diff 5 — G10 高一 (B2)
- gerund vs infinitive: "I enjoy ___ animals." (watching/to watch/watch)
- defining relative clauses: "The book ___ I read was great." (which/who/whom)
- present perfect continuous: "She ___ been studying for hours." (has/have/had)
- future continuous: "I ___ traveling tomorrow at noon." (will be/will have/am being)
- causative have/get: "I had my hair ___ yesterday." (cut/cutting/to cut)
- distinguish since/for: "I have lived here ___ 2010." (since/for/from)

### diff 6 — G11 高二 (B2+)
- participle phrase reduction: "___ tired, she went to bed early." (Feeling/Felt/To feel)
- inversion (negative adverb): "Hardly ___ I sat down when the phone rang." (had/did/was)
- mixed conditionals: "If I had studied, I ___ rich now." (will be/would be/were)
- non-defining relative clause: "My uncle, ___ lives in Tokyo, called me." (who/which/that)
- advanced subjunctive (proposal): "I suggest he ___ on time." (be/is/will be)
- reduced adverbial clauses: "When ___ a child, I loved music." (being/I was/I were)

### diff 7 — G12 學測 (C1)
- cleft sentences: "It was John ___ broke the window." (who/whom/which)
- advanced reported speech: "She asked ___ I could help her." (if/that/whether) [if/whether both ok — pick one]
- complex causatives: "She ___ the children play in the yard." (let/made/got)
- parallelism: "She likes hiking, swimming, and ___." (to climb/climbing/climb)
- emphatic do/did: "I ___ tell him yesterday, I promise!" (do/did/done)
- conjunctive adverbs: "He was tired; ___, he kept working." (however/although/despite)

### diff 8 — 分科測驗 AST (C1+)
- formal cohesion: "She loves chess; ___ , she dislikes checkers." (conversely/although/despite)
- subjunctive after expressions: "It is essential that he ___ on time." (be/is/will be)
- wherein/whereby: "The system ___ data is shared is broken." (whereby/wherein/whereas)
- nuanced concession: "___ his lack of training, he won." (Notwithstanding/Although/Despite of)
- inverted conditionals: "___ I known, I would have called." (Had/If had/Were)
- formal nominalisation: "The ___ of the new policy was unexpected." (implementation/implementing/implement)
